Course Overview

The course introduces students to selected topics in advanced quantitative research with applications in economics, political science, and environmental science. The aim is to deepen students' understanding of how to connect a research question to a logical argument, testable hypotheses, and appropriate statistical models.

It also focuses on developing the nuanced ability to assess the limitations of advanced models and techniques when answering substantive research questions. The course builds on basic inferential theory and linear regression, and introduces time series analysis and choice models.

01 — Theory & Inference

Deepen understanding of the connection between logical argument, testable hypotheses, statistical models, and causal inference.

02 — Application

Apply techniques in substantive research contexts and critically assess their limitations for answering real research questions.

03 — Programming

Develop statistical programming skills in R, including graphical display of data and more complex data management.

04 — Communication

Present quantitative findings clearly and rigorously to both academic and non-specialist audiences.

AQRM Replication Project

The centrepiece assessment of the course is a replication project in which student groups reproduce the quantitative findings of a published social science paper and present their work as a research poster. The project develops both technical skills and critical engagement with the published literature.
